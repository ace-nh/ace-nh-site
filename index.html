About ACE NH

Building Futures in Architecture, Construction & Engineering

ACE Mentor Program of New Hampshire is a nonprofit 501(c)(3) after-school initiative that gives high school students direct access to working professionals in the AEC industry. Students meet weekly during the school year, collaborating with mentors from leading NH firms on real-world design challenges.

At the end of each year, students present their projects to industry leaders, government officials, and families — and compete for scholarships and stipends toward college or trade school. The program is completely free for students.

Scholarships Available

Students compete for scholarships and trade school stipends at year-end presentations.

Industry Mentors

Volunteer professionals from 70+ NH firms guide student teams through real projects.

Nationwide Network

Part of ACE Mentor Program of America — a 30+ year national nonprofit with 70+ affiliates.

Real-World Projects

Students use Revit, tour job sites, and work on new design challenges each year.

The Program

How ACE NH Works

A structured school-year experience that goes far beyond the classroom.

1

Apply & Register

High school students from participating NH schools register each September. The program is free — no cost to students or families.

2

Weekly Sessions

October through February, teams meet weekly (2:30–4:00 PM) with industry mentors to work on that year's design challenge.

3

Field Trips & Site Visits

Students tour active job sites, visit Autodesk, and attend industry events — building real connections and context.

4

Present & Earn Scholarships

March culmination: teams present their work to judges, government officials, and sponsors — and compete for scholarships.

Participating Schools: Winnacunnet, Portsmouth High, Manchester School of Technology
